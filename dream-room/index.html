Dream Room:
Designing for peaceful feelings

Three years ago I went to Plan B, a new activist festival in Kharkiv, Ukraine. It was held in an old circus: majestic, gilded, and peeling apart at the edges. Everything felt urgent and raw and exciting. There were so many faces and smells and lights and so much noise.

By the end, I was exhausted.

The closing party was in a subterranean club. You could hear the urgent techno from the door.

Inside, relentless strobe-lit bodies pounding the ground. The air was wet with energy. Sweaty dancers were thrashing, pulsing, and flailing.

But in the top corner of the club, a single table. Dreamy candle-lit faces encircling the table. And the host gently pouring each person some tea.

It was perfect. It was a different kind of space inside the space. It was a space to find peace without retreat.

That's when I knew I would build spaces like this: spaces designed for peace inside the party.

Design: The Dream Room

I designed and conceived of the dream room collaboratively with Brie Code and Linsey Reymakers. We launched it at A MAZE. Berlin.

An overview of a space we designed for A MAZE. Berlin

What is the Dream Room?

The Dream Room is a warm, soft parallel to the party and magical chaos of A MAZE. It is a secret space that you can slip away into, a space that is part of the party--but so much softer.

The Dream Room is a space with soft music, soft lights, soft surfaces, and warm tea. It is a ritual space for people to connect, and to reflect on the happenings at A MAZE. and the spaces between them.

Soft cushions, sheer curtains and heated blankets surround an interactive, slowed-down, softened live-feed video projection of some of the (otherwise hectic) hotspots of A MAZE. At the centre of the space is a tea service with an ongoing tea ritual.

Why Does A MAZE. need a Dream Room?

All of us need to take a breath from time to time. Even the wildest party friend eventually needs a retreat from the flashing LEDs, moshing friends, and chiptune orchestras.

And our dreamers and thinkers and softies and quiet weirdos need a space to unfurl, to daydream, and experience A MAZE. in their own way. The Dream Room is a party for the gentlest partiers. It’s a way for people to stay at A MAZE., to be part of A MAZE., but to take some soft time for themselves.

The Dream Room allows anyone to slip through a secret doorway into a softer party, and to emerge again with replenished energy.

Tech Details/Logistics

We need to listen responsively, opening ourselves to a variety of listening tactics: stories, metaphors, images, play, data, interviews, and on-site interactions. Experiential truth is something that needs to be experienced to take shape.

We must be consent-driven in our practice, continuously open to our unexamined bias, deeply skeptical of all speculative empathy, and diligent in our validation.

A few questions to consider:

  • We will need power and heat. The steady and constant warmth of the space is absolutely critical to the concept. Space heaters and blankets should work.
  • We will need to mount a projector up high.
  • The tent should fit at least 8 people stretched out and lounging in a circular shape. The seating should encourage lounging.
  • The installation will be a simple, slowed down, distorted live-feed of A MAZE. hotspots (dance floor, exhibition, talks). Time permitting, there will be an interactive element, a peaceful participatory local multiplayer that is very simple (e.g. drawing digital fish and releasing them into the tea pond).
