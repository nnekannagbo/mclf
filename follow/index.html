Follow
A two-player cooperative game

Follow explores the ways we move together. Follow is a cooperative game about the intimacy of opposition, and the delicate balance of shared control.



The basic structure is:

  • Two players, two enormous LED buttons (each player controls one button)
  • On the screen, some very minimal circles (representing players) surrounded by rings (representing energy)
  • The left button can only pull both onscreen players left, and the left player starts losing rings/energy to the right player
  • The right button can only both onscreen players right, and the right player starts losing rings/energy to the left player
  • If both press their buttons everything momentarily pauses in place
  • If either player runs out of rings the game ends

There are also some falling mini-spikes that "pop" rings and falling rings that players can catch.

Follow continues a thread that came out of my earlier games: Closer, a game where the single playable controller is cooperatively and continuously shared between two players and Common, where the entire game emerges from the stewardship and maintenance of the relationships of the players.

With follow I was thinking about the nature of opposition, leading/following, shared control, and balance.

Thanks to the NYU Game Center and the No Quarter Commission that made this possible. Thank you to Arnab Chakravarty for collaborating on the controller: everything from cutting the box, to being a wonderful collaborator, to contributing Arduino code.
