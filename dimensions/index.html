Dimensions of Online Spaces:
10 Considerations when choosing or designing online spaces

A year ago when the pandemic pushed us online all the time, I made a list of standard video tools for meeting with others online.

Since then, I’ve make a list of more experimental spaces for online hangs, hosted meetups where we explore online collaborative spaces together, and even shared some of these spaces on national morning news radio.

In this work I’ve noticed some interesting dimensions in spaces for connecting online. These dimensions may be useful for people who are designing online spaces. They may also be useful to anyone who is considering what kinds of spaces they want to use or join.

I try to give extreme examples to show the edges of the dimensions, but they are mostly spectrums of possibility, rather than binary oppositions.

Dimensions of Online Spaces

Spontaneous <<<>>> Scheduled

  • Spontaneous Spaces allow you to show up at any time. Example: on Omegle (nsfw), you can hop on and connect with strangers who happen to be online any time.
  • Scheduled Spaces are designed for gatherings that happen at specific times. Example: a weekly Zoom reading group.

At the heart of this dimension is the tension between spontaneity and scheduling. Event organizers often prefer scheduling gatherings so they can more carefully manage attendees, navigate scheduling, and avoid security issues. Scheduled events can allow for planning, consistency, building tight communities, and building anticipation.

Meanwhile, many attendees want to manage their social energy in real-time, deciding moment-to-moment what they have the energy and capacity to do. Desire is growing for online spontanous spaces that resemble offline spaces like pubs, swimming pools, arcades, libraries and flexibly meet the real-time needs and desires of people.
